Music Reviews

  • Drinks Hippo Lite

    Produced within the space of a month in Southern France, Hippo Lite, the second album from the Tim Presley/Cate Le Bon partnership is an engaging, albeit inconsistent, mash of folk, field recordings and oddball’ism.

    Sean Caldwell reviews...

  • Hinds I Don't Run

    The Madrid four piece return with another breezy garage rock album to make you fall in love.

    Matthew Smith reviews

  • Sloan 12

    The Halifax, Nova Scotia veterans' twelfth record continues their firm, unpretentious streak of velvety power-pop.

    Juan Edgardo Rodríguez reviews...

  • Amen Dunes Freedom

    The Brooklyn singer-songwriter's fifth record fully exposes his experimental and conceptual faculties.

    Juan Edgardo Rodríguez reviews...

  • Sunflower Bean Twentytwo in Blue

    The Brooklyn trio finds liberation in music on Twentytwo in Blue. It's a noble aspiration for the Brooklyn trio, whose affection for classic rock resounds with a timeless spirit.

    Juan Edgardo Rodríguez reviews...

  • The Decemberists I'll Be Your Girl

    I'll Be Your Girl finds The Decemberists excellently incorporating electronic elements, adding a new color to their palette. 

    Joe Marvilli trips along with The Decemberists...

  • Yo La Tengo There's a Riot Goin' On...

    On their fifteenth album, the longstanding indie rock trio wants us to absorb their calm serenity.

    Juan Edgardo Rodríguez reviews...

  • Editors Violence

    One of the stalwarts of 00's post-punk revival scene return with a sixth album that will make you roll your eyes and tap your feet.

    Matthew Smith reviews

  • Lucy Dacus Historian

    The Richmond, Virginia singer-songwriter's Matador debut is a solid indie rock record: enjoyable, though not particularly memorable.

    Gabbie Nirenburg reviews...

  • Young Fathers Cocoa Sugar

    On Cocoa Sugar, Young Fathers use antagonism as a source of inspiration. The trio often assumes fictitious roles for the sake of making a more forceful point, and when they step out of them, they go against any moral rectitude to sound off on their opinions.

    Juan Edgardo Rodríguez reviews...
